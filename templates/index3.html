Northern Night Valley

Reaches of the mind


here was something magical about that night. The air, unaccustomed to it, is so fresh that it crashes into your lungs with a pleasant ache. It was so peaceful and quiet. Here, among the endless mountains and forest rivers, among the tall firs, whose branches are strewn with fragrant snow, you will never hear even the echoes of chaos. You will never hear the violent deaths of innocents, the explosions, the desperate cries of children.




UA Army Girl

The Depths Of My Subconscious


Self-portrait. Oil on canvas, size: A3. Time: two weeks. It's a surprisingly quiet morning. You are standing knee-deep in snow in some clearing. In your eyes everything is as foggy as it is outside. You do not understand how you got here. You turn around slowly, your gut feeling danger, like a true predator. What drives you? Hunger? Bloodlust? Or maybe... Fear?

Kat Oleshko #9

Save Ukraine by Art


I was born in the central part of Ukraine, in a city called Kropyvnytskyi. When I was 13 my mom decided to move us to Kyiv. In 2014 I emigrated to Poland and since then, trips to the “motherland” became rare but also more priceless. As Walter Benjamin said: "the art of wandering" is a stable tradition of the emigrant. I feel elusive nostalgia and love for places that, perhaps, for the residents of the European Union will seem exotic.
